National Fuel Plan — Response Phases MBIE · Source: Civil Defence
1
Minor
● Current Phase
Normal or short-term supply shock. Fuel companies manage via commercial arrangements. Government monitors global conditions and identifies risks.
2
Moderate
If disruption continues, government intervention begins. The Fuel Sector Coordinating Entity is activated for real-time coordination between industry and government.
3
Major
Support supply rerouting, secure alternative sources, balance regional distribution, implement demand reduction. Priority supply for essential services.
4
Severe
Serious supply shortage. Priority fuel allocation to essential services: healthcare, emergency, food, freight, utilities. Voluntary demand reduction recommended.
