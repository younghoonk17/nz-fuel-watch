Current Situation Assessment

Short Term

While stocks are healthy overall, onshore levels have dipped below MSO minimums for the first time in the crisis, now relying on on-water stocks in the EEZ to meet legal obligations. MBIE assessed the situation as Phase 1 (Minor) as of 22 March 2026. 2 vessels are scheduled to arrive within 2 weeks.

Watch Points

Onshore buffer for all fuels is now negative. The Mar 23–29 window shows only 1 vessel with no diesel. This creates a reliance on the 30 Mar – 5 Apr window which brings substantial diesel and jet fuel (18.8d combined) to restore onshore levels. Tight monitoring of arrivals is required.

Global Context

IEA 400M bbl collective action is active. Brent crude remains around US$88/bbl. NZ is fulfilling obligations via oil tickets. Retail fuel prices remain elevated but stable. Shipping disruptions in the Middle East continue to be the primary risk factor. No panic buying reported.
